Elephant

Elephants are the largest land mammals, native to Africa and Asia, and are known for their massive size, intelligence, and social structure. These herbivores have distinctive features, including long trunks, large ears, and thick, wrinkled skin. Elephants live in tight-knit family groups, with females leading the herd and males generally living solitary lives after reaching maturity. They are crucial to their ecosystems, helping to shape landscapes and maintain balance by dispersing seeds and creating waterholes. There are two main species: the African elephant and the Asian elephant, each with unique traits and habitats.
